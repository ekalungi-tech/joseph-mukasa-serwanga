Meet Joseph

A leader shaped by service, faith and community.

Joseph Mukasa Serwanga is a Ugandan from Bwebajja Mawanyi Village in Wakiso District. He is a former President of the Rotary Club of Kitende and has spent years serving people in Bwebajja, Kitende and neighbouring communities.

His leadership is not based on promises alone. He has supported families in times of need, helped residents understand and defend their lawful rights, encouraged young people, supported education and participated in village development initiatives. His service has reached people of different backgrounds, incomes, religions and political views.

As an active Catholic at Kabula Muliro Parish, Joseph believes leadership is a responsibility to serve with humility. He also respects all faiths and seeks to lead every resident fairly.

The People’s Manifesto

One village. One vision. Shared responsibility.

This manifesto is a practical commitment to accountable local leadership. It recognises that village development requires residents, institutions, religious leaders, cultural leaders, businesses, youth, women and government agencies to work together.

01

Total security and community safety

  • Strengthen neighbourhood watch systems and lawful community policing.
  • Improve communication between residents, police, landlords and local leaders.
  • Map security lights, dark spots, unsafe paths and high-risk areas.
  • Promote household registration and responsible tenant records in accordance with the law.
  • Establish emergency contact channels for crime, fire, accidents and domestic danger.
02

Education for every child

  • Identify children who are out of school and work with families to return them to learning.
  • Mobilise scholastic materials and support for vulnerable pupils.
  • Promote reading clubs, mentorship, career guidance and digital learning.
  • Encourage parents to protect girls and boys equally from neglect and exploitation.
  • Build stronger cooperation between schools, parents, churches, mosques and community leaders.
03

Sports, talent and youth development

  • Support organised football, netball, athletics and other safe sporting activities.
  • Promote Bwebajja Sports Club Academy and other youth initiatives.
  • Create village talent days for music, drama, art, innovation and entrepreneurship.
  • Connect talented youth to coaches, mentors, clubs and potential sponsors.
  • Use sports to promote discipline, health, teamwork and protection from crime.
04

Health, sanitation and dignity

  • Organise periodic health outreaches with qualified providers.
  • Promote maternal health, child health, disability inclusion and care for older persons.
  • Strengthen hygiene, drainage, waste management and clean-water awareness.
  • Encourage voluntary blood donation and emergency preparedness.
  • Promote mental wellbeing, family stability and responsible support for vulnerable residents.
05

Jobs, enterprise and practical skills

  • Develop a village register of skills, trades, businesses and job seekers.
  • Link youth and adults to vocational training, apprenticeships and available opportunities.
  • Promote local purchasing so village businesses can grow.
  • Encourage responsible digital work, agriculture, crafts, construction and service enterprises.
  • Engage employers and investors on fair opportunities for qualified local residents.
06

Women’s groups and strong SACCOs

  • Support transparent women’s groups, youth groups and savings associations.
  • Promote financial literacy, record keeping and responsible borrowing.
  • Encourage pooled funds for business, emergencies and community projects.
  • Link organised groups to legitimate government and development programmes.
  • Protect members from fraud, exploitation and unaccountable leadership.
07

Roads, drainage and a clean environment

  • Document priority road, drainage, flooding, waste and sanitation problems.
  • Coordinate lawful communal action and advocate to responsible authorities.
  • Promote tree planting, cleaner compounds and protection of water channels.
  • Encourage proper waste collection and discourage illegal dumping.
  • Publish progress updates so residents can track action and pending needs.
08

Accountability and accessible leadership

  • Hold regular village meetings with clear agendas and recorded resolutions.
  • Provide fair hearing without discrimination by wealth, religion, tribe or party.
  • Maintain transparent communication on village priorities and public resources.
  • Create channels for complaints, suggestions and emergency support.
  • Work as a team with the LC1 committee, elders and residents.
09

Patriotism, culture and unity

  • Promote love for Uganda, respect for national laws and responsible citizenship.
  • Support the positive cultural and development efforts of the Buganda Kingdom.
  • Respect every resident regardless of religion, ethnicity or political affiliation.
  • Encourage peaceful dialogue and reject division, hatred and violence.
  • Build one village acting together for the common good.

The people who shaped him

A legacy of character, discipline and service.

Deo Mukasa Mentor photo • Age 90+

Mentor and elder

Deo Mukasa

Deo Mukasa, a respected elder of more than 90 years, has been a mentor to Joseph Mukasa Serwanga. Through his words and example, he taught Joseph character, integrity, respect for people, fear of God, humility and the meaning of servant leadership.

His wisdom remains a guiding light: leadership is not a privilege for personal gain, but a duty to protect, unite and serve others faithfully.

Late Mr. Serwanga Father’s memorial photo

A father’s discipline

The late Mr. Serwanga

Joseph’s late father, Mr. Serwanga, trained him in discipline, hard work and personal responsibility. He had no tolerance for laziness and insisted that Joseph read, study and apply himself until he became mature enough to understand the value of education for himself.

That firm foundation helped shape a man who values preparation, order, responsibility and perseverance—qualities he now seeks to place at the service of the whole village.

Frequently asked questions

About the campaign

What kind of leader is Joseph Mukasa Serwanga?

He presents himself as a humble, accessible and service-oriented leader who values teamwork, discipline, fairness and practical action.

Will he serve people from every religion and political party?

Yes. The campaign’s stated position is that village leadership must serve every lawful resident fairly, without discrimination by religion, tribe, income or political affiliation.

How will residents follow progress?

The manifesto proposes regular village meetings, documented priorities, public progress updates and accessible channels for complaints and suggestions.

How can I participate?

Residents can volunteer, attend meetings, share verified community needs, mobilise peacefully, propose practical solutions and vote on election day.
